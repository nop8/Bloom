Overview

Junji Ito is a Japanese horror manga artist renowned for works like Tomie, Uzumaki, and Gyo, blending body horror, cosmic dread, and surreal imagery. Initially a dental technician, he debuted in 1987 with Tomie, winning the Kazuo Umezu Prize. Influenced by H.P. Lovecraft, Kazuo Umezu, and Shigeru Mizuki, his Eisner Award-winning works have inspired global adaptations and cemented his status as a modern horror icon.
